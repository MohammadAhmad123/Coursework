Role-Playing Games (RPGs)

Role-Playing Games (RPGs) are a genre of video games where players assume the roles of characters in a fictional world, often engaging in quests, battles, and exploration. RPGs emphasize storytelling, character development, and decision-making, allowing players to customize their characters' skills, abilities, and appearance as they progress.

Key features include:

  • Character Progression: Players level up by gaining experience points, unlocking new abilities and equipment.

  • Story-Driven Gameplay: RPGs often have rich narratives, with players making choices that influence the game's story.

  • Exploration: Players explore vast worlds, interact with non-playable characters (NPCs), and complete missions or quests.

  • Social Interaction: MMOs encourage teamwork, often requiring players to form groups (guilds or clans) to complete difficult quests or challenges.


Popular examples include classics like Final Fantasy, The Elder Scrolls, and The Witcher series.

Massively Multiplayer Online Games (MMOs)

Massively Multiplayer Online (MMO) Games are a genre of video games that allow thousands of players to interact in the same virtual world simultaneously. MMOs often feature persistent game worlds where the game continues even when players are offline.

Key features include:

  • Large-Scale Multiplayer: Hundreds or thousands of players can cooperate or compete in real-time.

  • Persistent Worlds: The game world evolves and changes, with events occurring even when players aren't logged in.

  • Character Customization: Players create and develop unique characters, often leveling them up and acquiring gear over time.

  • Social Interaction: MMOs encourage teamwork, often requiring players to form groups (guilds or clans) to complete difficult quests or challenges.


Popular examples include World of Warcraft, Final Fantasy XIV, and The Elder Scrolls Online. MMOs blend social interaction with exploration, combat, and questing.

Looter Shooters

Looter Shooters are a subgenre of action games that combine fast-paced shooting mechanics with the addictive loop of collecting loot. In these games, players engage in combat while continuously finding or earning new weapons, armor, and gear that improve their character's abilities.

Key features include:

  • Loot Collection: Players constantly acquire random, tiered loot (weapons, armor, gear) from enemies, chests, or missions, with higher-tier items offering better stats or unique effects.

  • Fast-Paced Combat: Gameplay is often centered around first-person or third-person shooting, with an emphasis on skill and strategy.

  • Character Progression: Loot helps players improve their characters, making them more powerful as they face tougher challenges.

  • Multiplayer Focus: Often designed for co-op play, allowing players to team up for missions or raids.

Popular examples include Destiny, Borderlands, and The Division, where players are constantly chasing better gear and upgrades to improve their character's performance.
